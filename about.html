Hello!

I'm Giulio. I'm from Italy and I'm currently a PhD student in analytic philosophy at the University of Barcelona. In my academy research, I study what value is. Some of my work has been published in Theoria and Synthese (forthcoming!) and I have presented it at the 25th Oxford Philosophy Graduate Conference.
I've been in close contact with computers since young age — when I was around twelve I built a website with photos and videos of me and my friends skateboarding —, but it was only around 2015, when my English was finally pretty good and I was taking a break from philosophy, that I started studying programming seriously.
I first attended Harvard's Introduction to Computer Science (CS50) online. It was a quite tough introduction, but also extremely satisfying. Since then, even if I ended up continuing my academic path, I have never stopped learning about computers and programming.
I'm currently looking for opportunities as a software engineer (full-time or part-time). I would love to work in a place where I could be mentored by skilled engineers and learn deeply about web development and networking as well as lower level programming.
I'm excited about software in general, but, in particular, about software that can allow different people to interact fruitfully, knowledge-sharing platforms, and software that can assist individuals in their intellectual, artistic, and also physical, efforts. A big part of the fascination that software has over me is due to Free and Open Source Software and, in particular, to Emacs, which I use daily and has had a huge impact on the way I have conducted my academic research.
