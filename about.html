Hello!

I'm Giulio. I'm from Italy and I'm currently a PhD student in analytic philosophy at the University of Barcelona. In my academy research, I study what value is. Some of my work has been published in Theoria and Synthese (forthcoming!).
I've been in close contact with computers since young age — when I was around twelve I built a website with photos and videos of me and my friends skateboarding —, but it was only around 2015, when I was taking a break from philosophy and my English was finally pretty good, that I started studying programming seriously.
I first attended Harvard's Introduction to Computer Science (CS50) online. Completing it was tough but extremely satisfying. Since then, even if I ended up continuing my academic path, I have never stopped learning about computers and programming.
I'm excited about software that can allow different people to interact fruitfully, knowledge-sharing platforms, and software that can assist individuals in their intellectual and artistic efforts. A big part of the fascination that software has over me is due to Free and Open Source Software and, in particular, to Emacs, which I use daily and has had a huge impact on the way I have conducted my academic research.
